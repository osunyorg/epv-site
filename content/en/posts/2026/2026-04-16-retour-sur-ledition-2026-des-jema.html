On April 11 and 12, the Chamber of Commerce and Industry (CCI) Grand Nancy Métropole - Meurthe-et-Moselle vibrated to the rhythm of passion and controlled gesture.
On the occasion of the European
Crafts of Art (JEMA), the Réseau Excellence Grand Est has highlighted talent
exceptional of its craftsmen, confirming once again the vitality of this
sector in our territory.


For two days, the ICC compound was
transformed into a true open-pit factory. Between explanations
exciting and captivating demonstrations, the public, come in numbers, could
discover behind the scenes of trades where the hand of man dialogues with the
material to create the exceptional.

Les Ateliers Sacker & Masse

A showcase of the know-how of excellence
This event was marked by the presence of artisans
and renowned workshops that embody the identity of our network. We want to
Welcome the commitment and quality of the exchanges proposed by:

The second day was also honoured by the
visit Emma Schneeberger (project mission
trades) and Christine Lemarchand (Contracting and Eventing Mission) of the Grand Region
East. Their presence underlines the importance of institutional support in the
of our companies.

A catalyst for visibility and recognition for the Network
Beyond
of the meeting with the public, participation of the Réseau Excellence Grand Est
the JEMA is a major strategic lever for the life of our
association. In
investing in a place as emblematic as the ICC, the network considerably strengthens
its visibility and credibility with economic actors and
institutions. This event provides a unique forum to demonstrate the
added value of our labels and the strength of our collective, transforming
every demonstration in a concrete proof of our regional dynamism. This
enhanced recognition not only enhances the brand image of
our member workshops, but also to strengthen our role as interlocutor
for the development of crafts. In short, these days
act as a powerful notoriety accelerator, reaffirming that
excellence in our know-how is an essential driver of the local economy
and Living Heritage
"These two days have been much more than just
technical demonstrations; they have been the scene of human experiences
genuine. Seeing wonder in visitors' eyes is the most
Great reward for our craftsmen." A huge thank you to all stakeholders, partners
and visitors who contributed to the success of this edition. Together, let's continue
to make the art craft of the Great East shine!
