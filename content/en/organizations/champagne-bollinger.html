Since 1829, House Bollinger has embodied excellence and refinement in Champagne through exceptional craftsmanship passed down through generations. Every artisan at the House works with passion around their signature grape variety, Pinot Noir, to create cuvées of rare elegance, blending complexity and depth.

More than just a champagne, Bollinger is an experience-an emotion shared among true wine enthusiasts.

Expertise and Innovation

  • Reserve wines in magnums : Bollinger holds an exceptional collection of reserve wines stored in magnums (hundreds of thousands), used to enrich blends and ensure the complexity and consistency of its cuvées
  • Oenological Heritage : In 2010, the House discovered a treasure trove of old bottles in its cellars (some dating back to 1830), initiating a world-unique restoration and archiving project
  • B Corp Certification: In 2023, Bollinger became the first Champagne House to be B Corp certified, reflecting its commitment to sustainability and Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR)

Viticulture and Winemaking

  • Estate Management: Cultivation of 179 hectares of vineyards, the majority of which are classified as Grand and Premier Crus (Pinot Noir, Chardonnay, Meunier)
  • Oak Barrel Vinification: Partial vinification in oak barrels, a rare and traditional artisanal method.
  • Extended Aging: All cuvées undergo prolonged aging in cellars, far exceeding the requirements of the appellation
  • Blend Enrichment: Meticulous management of reserve wines in magnums to enhance the aromatic profile of the wines

Distribution and International Reach

  • Global Presence: Available in over 100 countries through prestigious channels (Michelin-starred restaurants, luxury wine merchants, grand hotels)
  • Cultural Icon: The official champagne of James Bond, reinforcing its elite and elegant brand image
  • Royal Recognition: Holder of a Royal Warrant to the British Crown since 1884
