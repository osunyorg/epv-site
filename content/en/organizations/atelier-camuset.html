Founded in 1983 by Yves Camuset, the decorative painting workshop Atelier Camuset has continually innovated over time.

This family business, awarded the "Living Heritage of France" label in 2015, collaborates with leading decorators worldwide. Serving exceptional locations, the workshop creates and restores sumptuous decorations, crafting both contemporary and traditional paintings using time-honored techniques.

From heritage restoration to apartment renovations for the most discerning clients, Atelier Camuset's portfolio includes the Egyptian galleries of the Louvre Museum, the Casino de Monaco, the Hôtel de Galliffet, the Hôtel de Beauharnais, the Hôtel Bourbon Condé, the Musée de la Chasse et de la Nature (Museum of Hunting and Nature), the Sorbonne, as well as numerous private mansions, presidential residences, restaurants, and luxury hotels.

Today, the workshop, managed by his daughter Clotilde Camuset, perpetuates the business while adding a touch of modernity. A young woman rich in a precious heritage, she is determined to anchor it in the present through new projects.
