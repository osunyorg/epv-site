Its history of more than 200 years is closely linked to that of the pinasses of the Arcachon basin. First by sail and then by motor, what was only a simple boat of work for fishermen and oyster farmers has become over time a pleasure boat characteristic of the basin. The Dubourdieu yard was the first to imagine the potential of this boat. After 6 generations of Dubourdieu, the site was left without a successor. Emmanuel and Béatrice Martin took up the challenge and bought the site in September 2000. They were able to maintain the level of quality and requirement that made the shipyard famous. Today the creations DUBOURDIEU 1800 divide the seas of the world.
