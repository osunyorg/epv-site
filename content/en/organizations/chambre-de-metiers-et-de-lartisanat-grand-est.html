Their action is structured around four main missions:

1. Identification and "EPV Incubator"

The CMA has established a dedicated EPV incubator. Its role is to identify artisanal small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs) that possess rare or exceptional expertise but have not yet obtained the label. The incubator helps them assess whether they meet the required criteria: economic heritage, mastery of traditional or high-tech techniques, and local geographic roots.

2. Support with the Application Process

Obtaining the EPV label is a demanding process (involving inspection by the SGS, on-site audits, and ministerial decisions).

The CMA supports artisans through:

  • Structuring the application file: Organizing and drafting the necessary documentation.
  • Audit preparation: Ensuring the company is ready for the on-site inspection.
  • Administrative follow-up: Managing the process until the final certification is granted by the Regional Prefect.
