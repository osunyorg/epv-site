Tradition and textile know-how since 1902

Created in 1902, the company MERO (Moulinage and Retorderie de l'Oise) is the heiress of a century-old textile know-how. Originally dedicated to the manufacture of artificial bristles, it takes its independence in 1926 and adopted its current name.
In 1929, it was taken over by Marcel Théard, marking the beginning of a family management that continues Today.
His son Michel Théard took over in 1964, then his son granddaughter Sophie Théard succeeds him in 2000, ensuring the continuity of a vision both traditional and innovative.

Located in Neuilly-en-Thelle, Oise, MERO se specializing in milling, rearrangement and preparation Of textile yarn. It transforms natural, synthetic or techniques to adapt them to the requirements of weaving, knitting or knitting industrial embroidery.

With its expertise, MERO also offers Customised services such as multi-wire winding or braided twisting, integrated into its production chain in 1995. The company is intended for a wide range of clients, including: from the traditional textile industry to the technical and export sectors. It puts a point of honor to offer resistant, stable and aesthetics, suitable for demanding uses while guaranteeing quality and mastered know-how.
