This great French specialty first appeared in the 19th century. Nicknamed "the Zinc", it is actually made of tin, a 100% natural material.
In 1930, Marcel Nectoux opened his studio on Rue de Charonne in Paris during the Art Deco period. This unique know-how has been passed down through three generations since then.
In 2011, the Nectoux workshops relocated from Paris to Dax, while maintaining a showroom in Puteaux, below La Défense. In 2019, Thierry Nectoux passed the torch to Maxime Dethomas. Today, the Nectoux Workshops are among the last French artisans to carry out this activity, providing customers such as the bistros, restaurants and breweries of Paris, as well as those throughout France and the rest of the world: Europe, the United States, Asia, Australia and the Middle East.
