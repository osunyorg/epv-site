La Malle en Coin is a French artisan company specializing in the restoration, sale, and custom manufacturing of antique luxury trunks.

Recognized for its exceptional craftsmanship (awarded the Entreprise du Patrimoine Vivant label), it works on prestigious pieces and also offers a wide selection of spare parts. It is the driving force behind the Luggage Museum in Haguenau, which showcases this unique heritage.

Core Activities

Luxury Trunk Restoration

Specializing in renowned brands (Louis Vuitton, Goyard, Moynat), the team restores travel trunks primarily dating from the 1800s to the 1950s.

Sale of Spare Parts

Through their highly specialized shop, they offer over 500 different rigid parts for sale: locks, clasps, nails, corners, handles, hinges, etc., both new and antique.

Custom-Made Trunks

For clients who do not own an antique trunk, they design new, custom-made trunks, crafted in the tradition of the old trunk makers, using a stock of authentic materials.

Expertise and Consulting

They offer professional appraisals for collectors, auctioneers, and notaries, and publish a buyer's guide to luxury trunks as well as a book on restoration.

The Luggage Museum

Since 2011, they have entrusted the city of Haguenau with the management of a personal collection of over 700 trunks, which has become the largest in Europe.
