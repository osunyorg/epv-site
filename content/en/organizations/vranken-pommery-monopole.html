Founded in 1858, Maison Pommery owes its international renown to Madame Pommery, who revolutionized the world of wine by creating the first "Brut" champagne in 1874.

Located in Reims, its spectacular estate houses 18 km of underground chalk pits (crayères) listed as a UNESCO World Heritage site. Under the leadership of the Vranken-Pommery Monopole group, the House perpetuates this tradition of boldness by transforming its caves into a major exhibition space for contemporary art, while producing high-precision cuvées recognized for their freshness and finesse.

Core Activities

  • Premium Champagne Production: Crafting renowned cuvées (Brut Royal, Apanage, Cuvée Louise)
  • High-end Oenotourism: Guided tours of historical chalk pits and the architectural estate
  • Contemporary Art Promotion: Organizing "Experiences Pommery," blending underground heritage with artistic creation
  • Sustainable Viticulture: Strong environmental commitment with dual VDC (Sustainable Viticulture in Champagne) and HVE (High Environmental Value) certifications
